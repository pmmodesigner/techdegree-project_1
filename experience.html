I started my design career clear back in middle school. For some reason, I always found myself attracted to the package design of the groceries that my papá and mamá brought home from the market.

Realizing that my high school offered a computer arts class, I signed up at once.

Delving into photo manipulation, typography, pre-press, and some identity work, I was fortunate enough to start competing with other high schools state-wide and nationally. In 2009, I was honored with a second place award in Advertising Design at the National Leadership and Skills Conference in Kansas City, MO.

I proceeded to college at Idaho State University with a major in mass communication with an emphasis in graphic design.

In college, I took up a job designing posters for student body functions. And after college, I was offered a position at a local print shop, solidifying my print and pre-press skills. Later, I worked for a sign studio in Sun Valley, Idaho—taking my print skills into the third dimension. Currently, I'm employed by a nationally ranked newspaper as a layout editor.

And now I'm ready to propell myself from a print space to a digital one, visually communicating for future clients with my newly acquired coding skills on websites and applications.

Yeehaw, ladies and gentlemen!
